Methods 2022 - Panel Questions

  • What is the most common mistake you have seen from newcomers to the industry like us?
  • If you had to pick the one most beneficial thing you did in your past that has led you to where you are now, what would it be?
  • Have you had to deal with imposter syndrome? If so how often and does it ever go away? Or does it keep morphing into new things?
  • What is something that you wish Computer Science students were taught in school?
  • What is your opinion on people moving from job to job throughout their career? It seems to be the new wave to ride, but how does it look to people with long-ish careers
  • Is it really an employees market right now? Should I be picky when looking for a job, or should I just be glad to get a few offers?
  • Do you view an applicant with a Master's degree differently than one with only a Bachelor's degree? Or is there no difference?
  • Should students rely on internship experience and previous schooling when applying for full time jobs, or should we have a collection of personal projects as well?
  • How do you promote trust and communication within your orginization?
  • You hear a lot about how grades don't matter, jobs teach you way more than college, etc. Since we're all committing to university, what do you see as the advantages of a degree in computer science?
  • What are your thoughts on freelance work vs sticking with one company?
  • How do you walk away from a tough day at work, or a problem that you couldn't solve?
  • At what point in your career did you feel that you had finally mastered the work that you do on a daily basis?
